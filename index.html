Short Bio

Greetings, ethical stalker. This is Rajiv (27 earth years later), a PhD student in the Computer Science department at Duke University. I received my undergrad in CS from IIT-Madras. For the first two years, I suffered painfully to keep up with my academics. However, discovering competitive programming and working on writing a Chess engine kinda revived my interest in CS. Subsequently, in the final two years I was fortunate enough to work with Prof. Chester Rebeiro for developing a Web server from scratch using Rust, and with Prof. Sutanu Chakraborty in writing this paper (which made me realize--Golly molly, maybe I could do research!). After that, I worked under the supervision of Prof. Partha Talukdar, in a budding ML startup, kenome.io, and at Google Research. After that, with an ample amount of mental jujitsu, self-doubt, and self-questioning, I landed in Duke's Ph.D. program. Here, I work with Prof. John Reif in the field of Natural Computing. In my free time, I like to read and indulge in sports or cinema. I am also an intermediate level Chess player, slowly trying to improve. I like talking to people, come say hi!
